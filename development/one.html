Can't find direction in life?

  • Trying out a lot of new things doesn’t always help you figure out what you want to do in life. It might sound like a good idea to take a gap year, travel the world, and collect experiences, but this can often lead to more confusion. When you try too many things without really thinking about them, your mind gets overloaded with random experiences, making it harder to decide what you truly want.
  • The way the mind works is a bit like a library. Imagine if you kept throwing books into a room without organizing them—just piling them up without any labels or order. After a while, the room would be full of books, but if you wanted to find one specific book, it’d be nearly impossible. That’s what happens to your mind when you have too many experiences but don’t take the time to think about them. It all just becomes a big, messy pile of memories and ideas.
  • The important part is reflection. After every experience, it’s good to sit down and ask yourself, “Did I like that? Was it meaningful? Do I want to do more of it?” This helps your mind figure out what matters to you and what doesn’t. Without this step, you’re just collecting more and more experiences without learning anything from them.
  • Nowadays, the problem gets worse because we’re surrounded by so much information all the time—scrolling on phones, watching TV, or browsing the internet. We’re constantly taking in new things, but we rarely stop to think about what we’ve learned or felt. This endless stream of information makes it even harder to figure out what we really care about.
  • Instead of trying a little bit of everything, it’s better to focus on something you already love or feel curious about. For example, if someone loves playing the piano, they could use their time to learn different styles of piano playing from around the world. This way, they’re exploring within something they’re passionate about, which helps them grow and understand their interests better.
  • The key is to go deep into something you care about rather than spreading yourself too thin across many random things. When you focus deeply, you not only discover what you truly enjoy, but you also feel a sense of accomplishment and joy.

Summary: Trying too many things can confuse you, but reflecting on your passions and focusing deeply on what you love brings clarity and fulfillment.
